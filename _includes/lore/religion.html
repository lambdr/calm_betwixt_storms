Religion

The primary gods of worship across Aerth are the Five Celestial Lords, though in slightly different forms throughout the different lands. For example, a dwarf, living in deep in a clanhold under the Wyrmbone Mountains might depict Lord Amarhus as a dwarven smith, lighting the sun in his forge. On the other hand, a human sailor might depict him as a harbormaster, shepherding the ships into port with the coming dawn. The Celestial Lords are listed in the table below.

Amarhus & Belet-Seri

Lord Amarhus rules the divine realm of Celestia with his wife Lady Belet-Seri. Along with Lord Ishtar, they created the world and all its inhabitants from Primordial Chaos. Both Amarhus and Belet-Seri are stewards of the Sun, ensuring it rises and sets each day, keeping the world in order. Together, they maintain civilization, with Lady Belet-Seri overseeing the home and hearth, and their safe-keeping, while Lord Amarhus’ purview is that of order, duty, justice, and strength. These gods have the most clerics of any deity, and all paladins serve them first, above the other Celestial Lords.

Ishtar

After creating the world with Amarhus & Belet-Seri, Lord Ishtar withdrew from the affairs of other gods, although he keeps a watchful eye on their creations. His focus is on protecting civilization from the dangers and Chaos that threaten to overtake it. Additionally, his keen perception has given him insight the workings of fate and destiny. In addition to his clerics, most rangers and druids pay homage to Lord Ishtar, even though their magics are more primal in nature, rather than divine.

Ea & Iluwer

Lady Ea and Lord Iluwer are the twin children of Lord Amarhus and Lady Belet-Seri. While less commonly worshiped than their parents, both are greatly respected in their own rights, Lady Ea for her compassionate guard of travelers and the sick and wounded, and Lord Iluwer for preserving knowledge and inspiring innovation and change.

Eresh

Lady Eresh is said to have been born from a living sunbeam that Lord Amarhus lost in a bet with an Archfae. In that sense, she is his daughter, although is associated with the Fae and inherits their love of trickery and making deals. She is worshipped by merchants, thieves, and con artists.

Marax

Lord Marax was the general of the Celestial Horde, who ascended to godhood after his masterful victories in the Dawn War. However, the wounds of that war have yet to heal, and their lingering effects manifest in storms and natural disasters that rock the world. He is worshipped by soldiers.

Nanshe

No one knows where Lady Nanshe came from, but stories abound of how Lord Iluwer lured her in from a far-off realm. Whether she gave magic to mortals or merely governs its use now is debated among scholars and theologians. It is said she prefers illusion to the other schools of magic, and is rarely seen in Celestia. She is worshipped by mages and occultists.

Nin

Lord Nin walks the long grey road, and collects the souls who leave the mortal world. Though death is a painful ending of a life, Nin teaches us that souls persist, and he shepherds them through the transition of planes, keeping undue influence at bay. While undead are loathed by all gods as an afront to the Divine, clerics of Nin are especially bent on destroying them and their creators.

Evil in the Cosmos

Lord Rakas and Lady Damgulna are Evil, and have worshippers amongst those who seek to harm and abuse others, but even good mortals may give them offerings to ward off their influence. Lord Rakas rules the Hells beneath the Celestial Mountain, and Lady Damgulna rules the colder, darker places beneath, where even Devils fear to set foot.

However, there are many other sources of Evil in the world that defy the Divine Order. Demons and the remnants of the Primordials loathe the gods and their creation, seeking to destroy all that exists. These Chaotic forces are an afront to the natural order of the Divine, and thus are not themselves gods. Demon Lords may have worshippers – such as goblins, gnolls, or drow – but this relationship is somewhat different from those of mortals and gods.

Notably, the Demon Prince Orcus is Lord of Undeath; undeath is a perversion of the natural order, it defies the gods and their creation by trapping the remains of a mortal (or in particularly terrible cases, that mortal’s soul) in between the living and the dead. Even Evil gods, such as Lord Rakas, abhor this act, but there are those in the world who still perform it.



Other Beings of Power

The gods are not the only powerful beings known to exist. Some, like the Archfae and Demon Lords mentioned above, gather their own followers and can exert a mighty influence – nearly godlike – in the world. Other powerful beings include certain celestials (i.e. Archangels) and devils, or even dragons who grow old enough.
